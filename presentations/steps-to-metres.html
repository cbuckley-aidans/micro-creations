The challenge

How far did you walk?

Turning your steps into metres.

Counting steps is the easy part. Turning them into metres is the clever bit.

1

The task

You are walking from Eunice Plaza to the main entrance of the S&T building. You want the micro:bit to count each step you take. When you press button A, it should show roughly how far you have walked, in metres.

The micro:bit can count steps easily.

But we did not ask for steps. We asked for metres.

2

The question

So how do we turn steps into metres?

The micro:bit only knows how many steps you took.

We need a way to swap a number of steps for a number of metres.

Let's start with one single step.

3

The key idea

One step does not quite fill a metre.

1 step ≈ 0.67 m

1 metre

One step covers about two thirds of a metre.

So it takes about one and a half steps to make a single metre.

4

Two ways to do it

Say you took 50 steps.

50 steps

in metres
about 33 m

Fewer metres than steps, because each step is less than a metre. Here is the sum, two ways.

Method 1

Divide by 1.5

50 ÷ 1.5 = 33.3 m
Method 2

Multiply by 0.67

50 × 0.67 = 33.5 m
5

Why roughly?

Everyone's stride is different.

A tall person's step is longer than a short person's.

0.67 m is a fair average, so the answer is a good estimate, not an exact measurement.
