What it does

print shows something on the screen.

You give it something. It writes that something out for you to read.

If you want the computer to tell you anything, you reach for print.

3

The four parts

print("Hello")
printThe name of the job. It means "show this".
( )The brackets. You hand print its job inside these.
" "The quotes. They wrap around text.
HelloThe message. The bit you actually want shown.
4

Quotes mean text

Quotes tell Python "this is words".

print("Good morning")

Anything inside the quotes is shown exactly as you typed it.

OutputGood morning

Notice the quotes themselves are not shown. They are just the wrapping.

6

Numbers are different

Numbers do not need quotes.

print(7)
Output7

Quotes are for words. A bare number is treated as a real number.

"7" with quotes is text. 7 without quotes is a value you can do maths with.

7

It works things out

print(5 + 3)

No quotes, so Python does the sum first.

Output8

It shows the answer, not the sum.

Wrap that in quotes and it changes everything. We'll test both.

8

Several things at once

Commas let you show more than one thing.

print("Score", 10)
OutputScore 10

Each comma adds a space between the parts. Text and a number, side by side.

9

Where it goes wrong

Three things that trip everyone up.

print(Hello)No quotes around the word. Python goes looking for something called Hello.
print("Hello"Forgot to close the bracket. Python is still waiting for the rest.
print("Hello')Opened with a double quote, closed with a single. They must match.
