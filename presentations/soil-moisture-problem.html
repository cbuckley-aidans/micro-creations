Soil moisture meter
0

The challenge

Does this plant need watering?

Building a moisture meter with a micro:bit.

The plant cannot tell us. So we will build something that can.

1

The task

You have a pot of soil and a micro:bit. Build a moisture meter that can sense whether the soil is dry or wet. If the soil is dry, the micro:bit should buzz so you know the plant needs a drink. Otherwise, if the soil is wet, it should show a happy face so you do not overwater.

Simple enough to say. But a micro:bit cannot feel wetness.

It can only work with numbers. So our first job is to turn moisture into a number.

2

The science

How can electricity tell us about water?

We push two metal nails into the soil and connect them to the micro:bit.

The soil sitting between the nails becomes part of an electric circuit.

Whether electricity can flow across that gap depends entirely on the soil.

3

The science

Soil resists the flow of electricity.

Pure water does not carry electricity well. The nutrients dissolved in it do.

More water means more dissolved nutrients moving about, so current flows more easily.

Dry soil blocks the current. Wet soil lets it through. Soil behaves like a variable resistor.

4

The reading

The micro:bit turns that flow into a number.

a wet pot reads high
0no current
1023max current

It reads the current as a number from 0 to 1023.

The more easily current flows, the higher the number climbs.

So dry soil reads low and wet soil reads high. Moisture has become a number.

5

Calibration

But what counts as dry?

dry ≈ 250
wet ≈ 1000
0no current
1023max current

A number like 400 means nothing on its own. It depends on your soil and your nails.

So test your own setup. Push the nails into dry soil and note the reading.

Then push them into wet soil and note that reading.

Now you have your scale. Dry sits low, wet sits high, and your soil decides the exact numbers.

6

The decision

Pick a line in the middle.

dry
wet
threshold
0
1023

Choose a value somewhere between your dry and wet readings. Call it the threshold.

Any reading below the line, the soil is dry.

Any reading on or above the line, the soil is wet.

That single comparison is the decision your program has to make.

7

A real constraint

A meter has to last.

If we measure constantly, the probes are always powered. That drains the battery and slowly corrodes the nails.

So we only power the probes for the instant we take a reading, then switch them off.

Soil dries out slowly, so we can also wait a few seconds between readings rather than checking thousands of times a second.

8

Your problem, broken down

What your solution must do.
