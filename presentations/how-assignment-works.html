Assignment

What the equals sign actually does.

It does not mean what it means in maths.

1

The puzzle

score = score + 1

In maths, this makes no sense.

Nothing can equal itself plus one.

So why is this line everywhere in code?

2

The big idea

The equals sign is not "equals".

score = 0

It means: take the value on the right, and put it into the box on the left.

score ← put 0 in here

3

Picture it

A variable is a box.

score

It holds one value at a time.

You can look inside it, or replace what is in it.

Replace the value, and the old one is gone for good.

4

Putting a value in

score = 0
score

Put 0 into the box.

score now holds 0.

5

Replacing a value

score = 10
score

Put 10 into the box.

The 0 is gone. A box only ever holds one thing.

6

Now the tricky line

score = score + 1
score

Rule: always work out the right-hand side first.

What is in score right now? It's 10.

score + 1  =  10 + 1  =  11

Now put that answer, 11, into the box.

score is now 11.

7

Run it again

score = score + 1
score

Run the very same line again.

11 + 1  =  12

12 goes into the box.

Every time this line runs, the number climbs by one.

8

The one rule

Right side first. Then put the answer in the box.

1. Work out the right-hand side.
2. Put that answer in the box on the left.

Remember that, and assignment will always make sense.
